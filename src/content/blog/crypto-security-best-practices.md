## Why is Crypto Security Important?

The decentralized and often anonymous nature of cryptocurrencies makes them a target for hackers and scammers. Unlike traditional banking, there's often no central authority to appeal to if your funds are stolen. "Not your keys, not your crypto" is a popular mantra for a reason.

## Essential Security Measures

1.  **Use a Hardware Wallet:** For significant holdings, a hardware wallet (like Ledger or Trezor) is the gold standard. It keeps your private keys offline, away from online threats.
2.  **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Secure your exchange accounts with an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator or Authy) instead of SMS-based 2FA, which is vulnerable to SIM-swapping attacks.
3.  **Beware of Phishing Scams:** Double-check URLs, be skeptical of unsolicited emails or messages, and never share your private keys or seed phrase with anyone.
4.  **Use Strong, Unique Passwords:** Create complex passwords for each of your crypto accounts and use a password manager to keep track of them.
5.  **Keep Your Software Updated:** Ensure your device's operating system, antivirus software, and wallet applications are always up-to-date to protect against the latest vulnerabilities.
